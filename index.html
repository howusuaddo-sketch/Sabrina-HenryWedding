Getting there & around

Transport

Fly into Kos International Airport "Hippocrates" (KGS). The venue is around 15 km / 20 minutes away.

Recommended

Hotel Transfers

Casa Paradiso doesn't run a free shuttle, but private or shared airport transfers can be arranged through the hotel's reservations team, so please pre-book before you travel. Call +30 22420 41488 or see the hotel's FAQ page.

Transfer details
Flexible

Car Rental

Rental desks are available at Kos Airport, and the hotel concierge can arrange car, scooter or bicycle hire during your stay. Parking at Casa Paradiso is free, which is handy for exploring the island.

Rental options at KGS
On demand

Taxis

Taxis can be ordered 24/7 through the hotel reception. Pre-ordering is recommended, as waiting times can reach 45 minutes at busy periods.

Budget friendly

Public Bus

A public bus stop sits very close to the resort with services to Kos Town during the day. Timetables are available from the hotel reception.

Explore Kos

If you're staying a few days, the island is yours. Here are our favourite ways to spend them.

Culture · 30 min drive

Kos Old Town & Harbour

Cobbled lanes, markets, tavernas and history at every turn. See Neratzia Castle, the Roman Odeon and the ancient plane tree where Hippocrates is said to have taught.

Map
Ancient wonder

The Asklepion

Kos's most famous site: a terraced ancient healing sanctuary just outside Kos Town, with sweeping views across the sea to Turkey.

Map
Golden hour

Sunset in Zia

A mountain village famous for the best sunsets on the island. Browse the little shops, visit the natural park, and stay for dinner with a view.

Map
Natural spa

Therma Hot Springs

A natural thermal spring that spills straight into the sea on a pebbled cove. Bring water shoes and go early to beat the crowds.

The venue's own stretch of coast: long, sandy and breezy, loved by windsurfers, with dunes, beach bars and gorgeous evening light.

Good to know

FAQs

When do I need to RSVP by?
Please reply by 1 February 2027 using the form above. If your plans change after replying, just let us know as soon as you can.
Can I bring a plus one?
We'd love to keep the day to the names on your invitation. If you're unsure who's included, just message us. We'd rather you ask than guess!
Are children invited?
Yes, little ones are very welcome, and Casa Paradiso is wonderfully set up for families. Please include them in your guest count when you RSVP.
What should I wear?
Elegant summer attire in the soft shades of our wedding palette. See the Dress Code section above for the colours. Light, breathable fabrics are a good idea, and please save white and ivory for the bride.
What will the weather be like?
Early June in Kos is beautifully warm, typically mid-to-high 20s°C during the day with mild, pleasant evenings. Sunglasses and sun cream by day; you shouldn't need more than a light layer at night.
Do I need a passport or visa?
Greece is in the EU. UK travellers don't need a visa for short stays, but your passport must be valid. As a rule of thumb, it should be issued within the last 10 years and valid for at least 3 months after the day you leave. If you're travelling from elsewhere, please check the entry requirements for your nationality in good time.
What currency and plugs do they use?
Greece uses the Euro (€). Cards are widely accepted, but a little cash is handy for tavernas and taxis. Plug sockets are the European two-pin type (220V), so pack an adapter.
How do I get around on the day?
The ceremony and celebration all take place at Casa Paradiso, so if you're staying there, there's zero travel involved. If you're staying nearby, we recommend pre-booking a taxi through your hotel. See the Transport section above for all the options.
